* 501 This Body

  :PROPERTIES:
  :GENRE:  poem
  :KEYWORDS:
  :CITE-KEY: FF:501
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

This body's birth
Helplessly
Smiles at the fast-approaching death.

This body's death
Ruthlessly
Roars at the fast-diminishing birth.

#+END_VERSE

  :PROPERTIES:
  :GENRE:  poem
  :KEYWORDS:
  :CITE-KEY: FF:503
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

O my desire-life,
Why do you force me
Always
To carry this heavy load
Of constant grief?
Why? Why?

O my aspiration-life,
I am dying for you.
I really am.
Where do you hide?
How do you hide?
Why do you hide?
Why? Why?

#+END_VERSE

* 535 Who Else Is Utterly Helpless?

  :PROPERTIES:
  :GENRE:  poem
  :KEYWORDS:
  :CITE-KEY: FF:535
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

Who else is utterly helpless,
If not I?
An urgent matter compelled me
To phone Heaven.
Heaven's line was busy, as usual.
I phoned earth.
Earth was not at home.
I phoned my soul.
My soul told me that
My problem was too complicated,
And I should not bother it.
I phoned my Lord.
My Lord said, "Son, when will
You learn the supreme secret:
'Self-help is the best help'?"
Now tell me, my seeker-friends,
Who else is utterly helpless,
If not I?

#+END_VERSE

  :GENRE:  poem

  :END:

With my inner Pilot,

With my inner Pilot,
He said to me,
"You are indeed My breathless Satisfaction."

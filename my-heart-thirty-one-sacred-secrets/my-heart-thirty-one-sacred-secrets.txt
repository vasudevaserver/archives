* My Heart Needs Only One Freedom

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:1
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
My heart needs only one freedom:
The freedom to dream.

#+END_VERSE


* A Depressed Heart

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:2
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
A depressed heart
Means a confessed failure.

#+END_VERSE


* Because You Are Always Depressed

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:3
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
Because you are always depressed,
God has secretly told His choice children
That you are not a member
Of His inner Family.

#+END_VERSE


* The Hour Of God

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:4
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
The Hour of God
Mercilessly threatens the doubting mind
And
Measurelessly enlightens the aspiring heart.

#+END_VERSE


* O Man Of Eloquence

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:5
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
O man of eloquence,
My mind sincerely admires you.
O man of silence,
My heart soulfully loves you.

#+END_VERSE


* The Farther Away I Am From My Inner Hunger

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:6
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
The farther away I am from my inner hunger,
The greater is my outer insecurity.

#+END_VERSE


* Before You Declare That You Have

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:7
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
Before you declare that you have
realised God,
Can you not tell the whole world
That God has truly and unconditionally loved
Your teeming ignorance?
Try it.
It may not be an easy task.

#+END_VERSE


* You Tell God

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:8
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
You tell God:
"My Lord, will You accept what I have for You-
My eternity's hunger-cry?"


God tells you:
"My child, will you accept what I have for you-
My Immortality's Feast-Smile?"

#+END_VERSE


* You Live For Yourself Alone

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:9
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
You live for yourself alone.
How do you then expect God to live with you
And any human being to die for you?

#+END_VERSE


* Alas

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:10
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
Alas,
My mind, the silly talker,
Has taken away all the wisdom-light
That I received from my Lord's
Silence-Eye.

#+END_VERSE


* Two Souls

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:11
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
Two souls
Meet to promise.
Two hearts
Meet to love.
Two minds
Meet to challenge.

#+END_VERSE


* The Quicker

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:12
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
The quicker
I unlearn,
The wiser
I become,
The better
I love my Beloved Supreme.

#+END_VERSE


* An Aspiring Heart

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:13
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
An aspiring heart
Is the only secret-sharer
Of my sacred soul.

#+END_VERSE


* My Lord

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:14
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
My Lord,
I do not want to carry the heavy burden
Of knowledge anymore.
I wish to carry the tiny briefcase
Of wisdom-delight
Which You have given me
As a blessingful gift.

#+END_VERSE


* Yesterday For The First Time

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:15
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
Yesterday for the first time
I loved my searching mind.
Today for the first time
I am loving my crying heart.
Tomorrow for the first time
I shall love my smiling soul.

#+END_VERSE


* During The Night

  :PROPERTIES:
  :genre: poem
  :keywords:
  :cite-key: MHOS:16
  :END:


#+BEGIN_VERSE

1.
During the night
I stay with my old and mature friends:
Dreams.
During the day
I stay with my young and immature friends:
Realities.

#+END_VERSE

  :END:

Soulfully pure.
